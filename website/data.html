Graph Data

by Joshua Lee and Mona Huang

What did we get our data?

The main source of data into our parallel Max-Flow Min-Cut implementation was images. Running Max-Flow Min-Cut no these images was the main part of the binary image segmentation process. Using images to generate graphs allowed us to easily create large data sets for our parallel Max-Flow Min-Cut algorithm. As discussed in the example above, a simple 18 mega pixel image can be converted into 2GB worth of data. We found most of our images on Google.com and wrote our own functions to convert these images into graphs.
